## Antony and Cleopatra

**Year:** 1824

**Summary:** "Antony and Cleopatra" by William Shakespeare is a tragedy that explores the intertwined themes of love and power. The play is set in ancient Rome and Egypt, where the passionate romance between the Roman general Mark Antony and the Egyptian queen Cleopatra unfolds against a backdrop of political conflict.

**Description:** In Antony and Cleopatra, one of William Shakespeare’s most celebrated tragedies, themes of love and power are woven into the dramatic setting of ancient Rome and Egypt.

## Hamlet

**Year:** 1603

**Summary:** In Shakespeare's famous tragedy, "Hamlet," a young prince struggles to get revenge for his father's murder. The story follows his difficult journey as he faces intense confrontations, leading to a dramatic conclusion that has captivated audiences for centuries.

**Description:** In this quintessential Shakespeare tragedy, a young prince's halting pursuit of revenge for the murder of his father unfolds in a series of highly charged confrontations that have held audiences spellbound for nearly four centuries.

## Romeo and Juliet

**Year:** 1597

**Summary:** "Romeo and Juliet" is a famous play by William Shakespeare about two young lovers, Romeo and Juliet, who come from families that hate each other. They fall deeply in love, but because their families are enemies, they have to keep their relationship a secret. Sadly, their love story ends in tragedy, and their deaths finally bring their feuding families together in peace.

**Description:** Romeo and Juliet is a tragedy written by William Shakespeare early in his career about two young Italian star-crossed lovers whose deaths ultimately reconcile their feuding families.

**Cover:** https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/id/8257991-L.jpg

## Macbeth

**Year:** 1508

**Summary:** "Macbeth" tells the story of a loyal general who secretly desires power. After hearing prophecies from three witches and being pushed by his ambitious wife, Macbeth murders the king to take the throne for himself.

**Description:** The play concerns a trusted general who secretly lusts for power. Encouraged by the prophecies of three witches and urged on by his ambitious wife Macbeth commits regicide.

**Cover:** https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/id/872432-L.jpg

## A Midsummer Night's Dream

**Year:** 1600

**Summary:** In "A Midsummer Night's Dream," two young couples flee into an enchanted forest to escape their troubles. Unbeknownst to them, the forest is home to mischievous fairies and hobgoblins who delight in playing tricks.

**Description:** One night two young couples run into an enchanted forest in an attempt to escape their problems. But these four humans do not realize that the forest is filled with fairies and hobgoblins who love making mischief.
